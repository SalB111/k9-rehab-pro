## 🎯 QUICK SELECTION GUIDE

### **ACUTE Stage (0-14 days post-op)**
**Focus:** Pain control, gentle ROM, prevent inflammation
- All Passive ROM exercises (EX-001 to EX-010)
- Cryotherapy (EX-200)
- Massage - Effleurage (EX-202)
- Aquatic - Deep Water (EX-112) *if incision healed*

### **SUBACUTE Stage (15-56 days post-op)**
**Focus:** Increase ROM, begin weight-bearing
- Passive ROM + Cookie Stretches (EX-020 to EX-022)
- Sit-to-Stand (EX-040)
- Static Balance (EX-091)
- Level Walking (EX-130)
- Laser Therapy (EX-204)
- NMES (EX-206)

### **CHRONIC Stage (57-180 days)**
**Focus:** Strength building, functional movement
- Three-Leg Standing (EX-042)
- Cavaletti Rails (EX-044)
- Backwards/Lateral Walking (EX-045, EX-046)
- Wobble Board (EX-092)
- Swimming (EX-111)
- Incline Walking (EX-131)
- Thermotherapy (EX-201)

### **RETURN TO FUNCTION (180+ days)**
**Focus:** Sport conditioning, high-level function
- Down-to-Stand (EX-041)
- Wheelbarrowing (EX-043)
- Figure-8 Walking (EX-047)
- Advanced Balance (EX-098, EX-099)
- Trotting/Jogging (EX-133, EX-135)
- Controlled Ball Chase (EX-134)

---

## 🚨 RED FLAGS - STOP EXERCISE IF:

- Pain score increases ≥2 points during exercise
- New lameness or favoring of limb
- Swelling, heat, or redness at surgical site
- Non-weight-bearing when previously weight-bearing
- Incision opens or shows discharge
- Patient refuses to perform exercise (may indicate pain)

---

## 📞 VETERINARY CONSULTATION REQUIRED:

- Pain level ≥8/10
- Non-weight-bearing beyond day 7 post-surgery
- Active infection or open incision
- Suspected implant failure
- Sudden loss of function
- Severe swelling or heat

## 🛡️ SAFETY NOTES

✅ **Always** get veterinary approval before starting any exercise program  
✅ **Monitor** patient closely for pain, fatigue, or distress  
✅ **Progress gradually** - increase by 10-20% per week maximum  
✅ **Ice after exercise** in acute/subacute stages (10-15 minutes)  
✅ **Stop immediately** if pain or lameness occurs  
✅ **Document** patient response to each exercise session
